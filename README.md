# Employee-Attrition-Prediction-End-to-End-Machine-Learning-Explainability-Report
# DATA : https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/pavansubhasht/ibm-hr-analytics-attrition-dataset
# 1. Data Preprocessing & Feature EngineeringDropping Invariant Features: 
During the Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA), we identified and removed four features: EmployeeCount, EmployeeNumber, Over18, and StandardHours. These columns       carried zero variance (identical values across all employees), meaning they would only introduce noise and unnecessary computational overhead without adding       any predictive value.Data Quality: The dataset was verified to be exceptionally clean, with zero missing values across all remaining features.Feature              Engineering (years_per_level): High multicollinearity was detected between MonthlyRate and JobLevel through a correlation heatmap. To capture a deeper             behavioral insight while resolving this, a new feature was engineered:$$\text{years\_per\_level} = \frac{\text{TotalWorkingYears}}                                 {\text{JobLevel}}$$Subsequently, the original JobLevel column was dropped. This new feature successfully measures corporate stagnation and, as proven later        by SHAP values, became a significant predictor of attrition.
# 2. Handling Class Imbalance (The EDA vs. Model Paradox)
The target variable (Attrition) exhibited a severe class imbalance, which is typical for HR analytics.The Analytics Paradox: Initial EDA (comparing feature        means between classes) suggested that certain features had weak individual correlations with attrition. However, once trained, advanced machine learning           models captured complex, non-linear relationships and multi-feature interactions that a basic univariate EDA could not reveal.The Solution: To prevent the         models from biasing toward the majority class (employees staying), SMOTE (Synthetic Minority Over-sampling Technique) was integrated directly into an              Imbalanced-Learn Pipeline. This ensured that synthetic data generation occurred strictly within cross-validation folds, completely preventing data leakage.
# 3. Model Performance & Business Trade-off
 sAfter optimizing Logistic Regression, Random Forest, and XGBoost via GridSearchCV using the F1-Score as the primary metric, we observed distinct behaviors        from each model:Logistic Regression + SMOTE (The Best Catch / High Recall):Recall: 0.659 | Precision: 0.356 | F1-Score: 0.462Business Application: This model      is aggressive at capturing potential resignations (detecting nearly 66% of actual attrition cases). It is ideal if the company’s priority is proactive             retention, where the cost of missing an employee who wants to leave is much higher than the cost of offering incentives to someone who intends to                  stay.XGBoost + SMOTE (The Conservative Predictor / High Precision):Precision: 0.750 | Recall: 0.319 | F1-Score: 0.447Business Application: This model is           highly precise; when it flags an employee as "Likely to Leave," it is correct 75% of the time. It is ideal if the HR department has a limited budget and           wants to intervene only when there is absolute certainty.
# 4. Model Explainability via SHAP ValuesTo bridge the gap between machine learning and human resources, SHAP (SHapley Additive exPlanations) was utilized to interpret global feature importance for both the highest-recall and highest-precision models:
   - A. XGBoost Insights (Focus on Decisive Triggers)
       1. OverTime_Yes (+0.54): By far the most critical factor. Working overtime is the strongest, most direct catalyst driving employees out of the company.
       2. StockOptionLevel (+0.43): Financial alignment and long-term incentives play a massive role in retaining top-tier technical talent.
       3. years_per_level (+0.23): Our engineered feature ranks in the top tier, proving that employees who spend too many years without moving up a job level are           highly susceptible to attrition.
   - B. Logistic Regression Insights (Focus on Holistic Environment)
        1. EnvironmentSatisfaction (+0.29) & JobSatisfaction (+0.24): This model relies heavily on workplace culture and employee sentiment, explaining why it                casts a wider net (higher Recall) but encounters more false positives.
        2. NumCompaniesWorked (+0.23): Employees with a history of job-hopping tend to maintain that pattern.
# 5. Strategic Recommendations for HR Management Based on the combined data science insights, the company can deploy two immediate retention strategies:
   1. The Overtime Audit (XGBoost Lesson): Immediately review departments with high overtime rates (e.g., Laboratory Technicians, which also spiked in XGBoost            SHAP). Overtime fatigue is the fastest track to attrition.
   2. The Stagnation Review (Engineered Feature Lesson): Utilize the years_per_level metric to flag employees who have outgrown their current job level duration          without receiving a promotion, and enroll them in career-progression pathways.
